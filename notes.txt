‚ù§Ô∏è 4. features/sentiment.py
Purpose:
Analyzes the mood (sentiment) of each post using VADER.
What it does:
score_text() ‚Üí gives each post a score between -1 (negative) and +1 (positive).
daily_sentiment() ‚Üí groups posts by day, then calculates:

Why it matters:
This converts messy human language into numeric features that can be tested against stock prices.

ANALYSES THE SENTIMENT OF EACH POST IN THE DATAFRAME AND ASSIGNS IT A SCORE FROM 1 (+VE) TO -1 (-VE)
CONVERTS THE LANGUAGE INTO A NUMERICAL SCORE TO BE COMPARED AGAINST STOCK STATISTICS (DAILY RETURN ETC.)




‚öôÔ∏è 5. backtest/strategy.py
Purpose:
Simulates a simple trading strategy based on the sentiment signal.
What it does:
Converts daily sentiment into positions:
+1 ‚Üí buy, -1 ‚Üí short, 0 ‚Üí stay out.
Shifts returns by one day (so you only act on yesterday‚Äôs data ‚Äî no cheating).
Applies trading costs (a few basis points per trade).
Calculates the running total of profit/loss over time (equity curve).
Why it matters:
This tests whether sentiment data gives a trading edge ‚Äî i.e., if ‚Äúbuy when people are happy‚Äù beats random chance.

SIMPLE TRADING STRATEGY TO LONG OR SHORT POSITIONS BASED ON THE SENTIMENT SCORE 
USES BASIS POINTS TO ACCOUNT FOR REAL LIFE SLIPPAGE AND EXTERNAL FEES 
TESTS IF THE SENTIMENT DATA HELPS WITH TRADING 


üìä 6. evaluation/metrics.py
Purpose:
Calculates key performance statistics for your strategy.
What it does:
Sharpe ratio: how much return you get per unit of risk.
Max drawdown: biggest drop from a previous peak (shows worst-case loss).
CAGR: compound annual growth rate (yearly growth if you reinvested profits).
Why it matters:
These metrics let you compare your sentiment strategy to a simple ‚Äúbuy & hold.‚Äù
